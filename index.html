BIO

Photograph by Elena Sloman

Luke Simonson Luke Simonson is an active freelance performer, woodwind specialist, and reedmaker based in the Twin Cities area. Though native to southern Minnesota, Luke has performed across the country and internationally in Spain, France, Italy, Argentina, and Uruguay. He has also appeared with the American Modern Orchestra, Mankato Symphony Orchestra, and St. Peter Choral Society.


Beyond oboe performance, Luke has performed as a woodwind doubler at Grace Performing Arts Center’s Festival 56, Kelsey Theatre at St. Olaf College, and the Paradise Center for the Arts. Some of his favorite productions include “Big Fish,” “The Hunchback of Notre Dame,” and “Honk.”


In addition to performing, Luke Simonson is dedicated to making and selling professional reeds that work throughout all seasons. His reeds offer depth of tone, stability in all ranges, and impeccable response. Luke currently maintains a private studio of young oboist in southern Minnesota.


Luke Simonson earned his MM in Oboe Performance and Literature from the Eastman School of Music, where he studied with Richard Killmer. He also holds an undergraduate degree from St. Olaf College, where he studied with Dana Maeda. In his free time, he enjoys petting his cat, cooking in his deep fryer, and learning computer programming languages.
